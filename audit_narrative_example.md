## Control Narrative

The organization maintains a formal logical access control program designed to restrict access to systems, applications, and data on a need-to-know, least-privilege basis. Access is provisioned through the organization's identity and access management (IAM) platform and requires documented approval from the relevant system owner prior to provisioning.

Privileged access rights — including administrative access to cloud infrastructure, production databases, and security tooling — are subject to an additional approval workflow requiring sign-off from the Head of Security. Privileged accounts are inventoried on a quarterly basis, with access removed promptly upon role change or termination. All privileged sessions are logged to the organization's SIEM and retained for a minimum of twelve months.

Access reviews are conducted semi-annually across all in-scope systems. Reviewers are provided with a current access roster and are required to certify, remove, or escalate each account within ten business days. Evidence of completed access reviews is retained in the compliance management platform for audit purposes.

Multi-factor authentication (MFA) is enforced for all user accounts accessing production systems and is a mandatory requirement for all third-party and contractor access. MFA enforcement is validated through automated policy checks run on a weekly basis.
